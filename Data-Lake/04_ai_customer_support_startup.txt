NovaCare was a small health-tech startup that provided an online platform for booking clinic appointments. Ban đầu, đội hỗ trợ khách hàng chỉ có sáu người, nhưng mỗi ngày họ phải xử lý hàng trăm tin nhắn về lịch khám, phí dịch vụ, hồ sơ bệnh nhân và cách sử dụng ứng dụng. During peak hours, users often waited more than twenty minutes for a response, which led to complaints and canceled bookings.

In early 2026, the company decided to build an AI-assisted customer support system. Mục tiêu không phải là thay thế toàn bộ nhân viên, mà là giúp họ trả lời nhanh hơn và nhất quán hơn. The system was trained on internal FAQ documents, clinic policies, appointment rules, and anonymized examples of previous support conversations. It could suggest draft replies, classify user intent, and retrieve relevant policy sections for human agents.

The first version worked well for simple questions, such as how to reset a password or how to cancel an appointment. Tuy nhiên, hệ thống gặp khó khăn với các trường hợp nhạy cảm, ví dụ người dùng hỏi về triệu chứng bệnh hoặc yêu cầu tư vấn y tế cá nhân. Because of this, NovaCare added strict rules: the AI could not provide diagnosis, recommend medication, or make medical decisions. Instead, it had to route such cases to licensed medical staff or advise users to contact a clinic directly.

The support team also introduced a review dashboard. Nhân viên có thể xem câu trả lời AI đề xuất, chỉnh sửa nếu cần, và đánh dấu câu nào không phù hợp. These corrections were later used to improve retrieval quality and update the knowledge base. After two months, average response time dropped from twenty minutes to five minutes, while customer satisfaction increased by 18 percent.

Still, the company learned that AI support required continuous maintenance. Policies changed, clinic schedules changed, and some answers became outdated quickly. Vì vậy, NovaCare assigned one operations manager to review the knowledge base every week. The final lesson was clear: AI can make customer support faster, but only if humans keep responsibility for quality, safety, and updates.
