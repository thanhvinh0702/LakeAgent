In the northern mountain village of Ban Lien, farming has always depended on seasonal rain, cool air, and traditional knowledge passed from one generation to another. Người dân nơi đây trồng lúa nương, chè Shan tuyết, ngô và một số loại rau chịu lạnh. For many years, the farming calendar was predictable: land preparation began after the first spring rains, tea harvesting peaked during the misty months, and corn was planted before the hottest part of summer.

Over the last decade, however, farmers have noticed unusual changes. Mưa đến muộn hơn, nhưng khi mưa thì thường mưa rất lớn trong thời gian ngắn. Some fields that used to stay moist until late summer now dry out by June. In other areas, sudden floods wash away young plants before they can take root. Người già trong bản nói rằng họ chưa từng thấy thời tiết thay đổi nhanh như vậy trong thời trẻ.

To adapt, the village cooperative started working with agricultural researchers. They tested drought-resistant corn varieties and introduced contour farming to reduce soil erosion. Một số hộ chuyển một phần diện tích từ ngô sang cây dược liệu như đương quy và atiso vì các cây này có giá trị cao hơn và phù hợp với đất dốc. The cooperative also built small water tanks near tea fields, allowing farmers to store rainwater during heavy showers and use it during dry periods.

The adaptation process was not easy. Many farmers were worried that new crops might fail or that changing old methods would be too risky. Vì vậy, hợp tác xã không yêu cầu mọi người thay đổi cùng lúc; họ chọn 12 hộ làm mô hình thử nghiệm trong hai vụ đầu tiên. After seeing better soil retention and more stable income, more households joined the program.

By 2026, Ban Lien had not completely solved the climate problem, but it had become more resilient. Tea quality improved because farmers used water more carefully, and crop diversity reduced the risk of losing everything in one bad season. The village learned that adaptation is not about abandoning tradition; it is about adjusting traditional knowledge to survive under new conditions.
